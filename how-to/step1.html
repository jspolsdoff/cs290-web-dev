What is Ruby on Rails?

So if you’ve never heard of Ruby on Rails or Rails for short I’ll give you a brief explanation of what it is.

Rails is a popular web application framework that uses the model-view-controller (MVC) architectural pattern and the Ruby scripting language. To put it simply it allows you to organize your application in a uniform manner and separate it three different modular parts: the model, view, and controller.

A good visualization of the MVC architechural pattern.

Model – represents the data in your application

Controller – sends commands to the model or view based on the user’s interaction with application

View – this is what the user sees on their web browser

The MVC pattern can be related to watching TV. As you are sitting on your couch you interact with your TV with a remote (controller) to change channels. The signal is sent from the remote to your cable providers box (the model). Finally your cable provider serves you the selected channel on your TV’s screen (the view).

Well there you have it. I very brief overview of Rails and MVC.
